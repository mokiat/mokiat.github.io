If you lower your OS X volume level to the lowest possible setting by using the volume down button, you get the following icon.

![Lowest Volume Level]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/volume-level-lowest.png)

If hit the volume down button once more you would get to the mute level. Which happens to look like so.

![Mute Volume Level]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/volume-level-mute.png)

Now, if you hit the mute button (in essence to unmute), it will bring you to this secret volume level.

![Mute Volume Level]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/volume-level-trick.png)

It does not have the backslash symbol running through the icon, so it's not mute.
Then again, the volume level is presumed to be at zero, since there are no bars to indicate otherwise.
In reality, the volume level you get is somewhere in between mute and lowest standard setting.

### Note

This works on my Mac Book Pro with `Sierra`, and I am pretty sure it did also with `El Capitan`. I believe I've seen it work on iMac as well, though I can't be sure.

To be honest, I have never really used it in my daily activities. So don't hold me responsible if this happens to mess your system, as unlikely as this may seem.
